Unit 1: Data (Simple Terms)

  • Bit: The smallest piece of data, either 0 or 1.
  • Byte: A group of 8 bits that form a small chunk of data.
  • Analog Data: Data that changes smoothly, like sounds or colors.
  • Lossless Compression: A way to shrink data without losing any information.
  • Lossy Compression: A way to shrink data by losing some details.
  • Metadata: Information about data, like a label or description.

Unit 2: Computer Systems and Networks (Simple Terms)

  • Computing Device: A machine like a computer or smartphone that runs programs.
  • Computer Network: Devices connected to share data.
  • Protocol: Rules that devices follow to communicate.
  • Bandwidth: How much data can be sent at once.
  • Router: A device that directs data along the fastest path.
  • URL: The website address you type in, like www.example.com.
